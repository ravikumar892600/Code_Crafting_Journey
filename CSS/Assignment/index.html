Ex Machina is a 2014 science fiction psychological thriller film. It was written and directed by Alex Garland, and stars Domhnall Gleeson, Alicia Vikander, and Oscar Isaac.The film is about a young programmer who is selected to participate in an experiment in synthetic intelligence. The programmer must evaluate the human qualities of a highly advanced humanoid A.I.

The title Ex Machina comes from the Latin phrase "Deus Ex-Machina," which means "a God from the Machine". The phrase originated in Greek tragedies, where an actor playing a God would be lowered down via a platform (machine) and solve the characters' issues, resulting in a happy ending.
